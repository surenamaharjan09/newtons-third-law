Newton's Third Law of Motion

"For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction."

Forces always come in pairs — acting on different objects, equal in magnitude, opposite in direction.

Key Facts

1. Forces come in pairs
If object A pushes object B, then B pushes A back with the same strength — but in the opposite direction.
2. The forces act on different objects
That's why they don't cancel out when analyzing the motion of just one object (e.g., you don't stay still when walking).
3. Rockets work in space because of this law
Hot exhaust gas is pushed backward (action) → rocket is pushed forward (reaction). No air is needed!

Rocket pushes hot gas downward (action) → gas pushes rocket upward (reaction)

NASA diagram: Exhaust pushed backward → engine/rocket pushed forward

4. Walking / Running
You push backward on the ground → ground pushes you forward (via friction). Without this reaction force, you'd slip!

Runner pushes backward/down on ground → ground pushes forward/up on runner

Foot exerts force on ground → ground exerts equal & opposite force on foot

5. Swimming & Balloon rockets
Push water backward → move forward. Same principle as letting air out of a balloon — it flies the opposite way!
6. Hitting a wall hurts
Your hand pushes the wall → wall pushes back equally hard (why it stings!).
